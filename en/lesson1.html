Roots

There are two types of root words in kawaba - semantic primes, and semantic molecules. All semantic molecules can be derived by compounding the semantic primes.

The semantic primes have a consonant-vowel (CV) structure.

The semantic molecules have a consonant-vowel-n (CVn) structure.

Try finding the word {"pe"|good} in the table! (Hover over the word to see if you got it right!)

How about {"won"|laugh}?

Important!

The root words can take on any part of speech. {"min"|eat/drink} can mean "to eat/drink", or "food/drink".
